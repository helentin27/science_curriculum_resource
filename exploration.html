The Discovery of Life Science
Exploration

In this stage, I acted as a facilitator to guide children with thoughtful and open-ended questions, in order to expand their curiosity. I first observed and jogged down children"s prior knowledge about animals" skin. They talked about

  • What they saw in the zoo and how some of the animals have fur and some don"t.
  • Animals in different sizes
  • Animals have long hair, spiky hair, short hair and no hair
  • Snakes and fish have scales

While children were sharing their prior knowledge and experiences, they decided to move one step further and to create a chart. In this chart, animals were separated into two categories: Animals have fur and Animals have no fur.

Then, I asked them, "Why do you think animals have different types of skin?" Then, one child responded, "I don"t know." Then I added, "Do you want to find out?" and they all agreed. At that point, we were all committed to the next process called investigation.
